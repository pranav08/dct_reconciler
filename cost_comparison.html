The Architecture Pays for Itself

The savings aren't from clever prompts or smaller models. They're structural. The LLM is asked to do exactly one job — parse messy text into a Pydantic model — and is sandwiched between deterministic Python on both sides. Every other operation (LOINC lookup, unit conversion, range checks, deduplication, validation) is plain code. The judge LLM is summoned, not on retainer. Cost discipline falls out of the design.

And so does compliance. Every layer emits a SHA-256-chained audit event into an append-only log, mapping directly to ALCOA++ (Attributable, Legible, Contemporaneous, Original, Accurate, Complete, Consistent, Enduring, Available) and 21 CFR Part 11 (§11.10(a–e), §11.50, §11.70, §11.200). Total compliance code: ~120 lines of Python.
